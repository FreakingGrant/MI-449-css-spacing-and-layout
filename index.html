See, usually paragraph tags are block elements, meaning that every paragraph tag in the page gets stacked on top of each other. They get their own lines. But what I did here is I wrapped them in a div tag, and then changed that div tag's display property to be inline-block. Now, this current paragraph won't be set on a new line, but rather right after the previous element, assuming that previous element is also displayed with an inline-block property.
